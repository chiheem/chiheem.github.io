About me

Hi! I am a Computer Scientist developing artificial intelligence (A.I.) and data science tools and applying them to a myraid of real-world problems in finance, supply-chain, and other areas. My current projects involve creating and evaluating Large Language Model (LLM) agents, and separately, developing new Graph Neural Network (GNN) architectures.

I am currently a Senior Research Scientist at Hitachi America, Ltd. I am also a visiting scholar at the Stanford Computer Science Department working on LLMs with Percy Liang and a research affiliate at the MIT Laboratory for Financial Engineering (LFE).

I hold a B.Eng (First Class Honors) in Mechanical Engineering and a B.SocSci (First Class Honors) in Economics from the National University of Singapore, as well as an S.M. and a Ph.D in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. My doctorate thesis was advised by Andrew W. Lo.
