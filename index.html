About me

I develop artificial intelligence and data science tools and apply them to a myriad of real-world problems in finance, supply-chain, and other areas. My current research involves benchmarking multimodal foundation models and creating generative AI agents. I also dabble in Graph Neural Networks (GNN) and physics-based neural networks. Check out my publications if you are interested.

I am currently a Principal Research Scientist at Hitachi America, Ltd. I am also a visiting scholar at the Stanford Computer Science Department working with Percy Liang.

I hold a B.Eng (First Class Honors) in Mechanical Engineering and a B.SocSci (First Class Honors) in Economics from the National University of Singapore, as well as an S.M. and a Ph.D in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. My doctorate thesis was advised by Andrew W. Lo.
